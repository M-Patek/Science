# Hypothesis — self-bootstrap effectiveness cohort v2

For twelve frozen task blocks, the structured-treatment arm will exceed the solo-control arm by at least
0.5 points in the mean within-block adjudicated quality difference (0–10). Its median within-block total
elapsed-time ratio will be at most 1.25 and the cohort will contain zero critical violations.

Exactly 24 ordinal-1 cells are planned: one control and one treatment cell per block. There are no
result-dependent replacements. The joint claim is `supported-for-frozen-fixtures` only when all twelve
pairs are evaluable and every threshold passes. A missing pair or any critical violation makes the joint
claim inconclusive; otherwise a threshold failure falsifies it.

This is prospective for v2 outcomes but outcome-informed by earlier engineering pilots and the abandoned
v1 designs. It is not a claim about model populations. Any conclusion is limited to the frozen fixtures,
baseline revision, declared tool policy, and host-observed unsigned runtime labels.
